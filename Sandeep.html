01. HOSPITALITY (Heaps)

PROBLEM: Linear sorting of customer wait-times is O(N log N). In a busy food zone, this lag causes queue abandonment.
SOLUTION: A Min-Heap allows O(1) access to the empty table. This maximizes table turnover rate and revenue per hour.

02. FINTECH (KMP Algorithm)

PROBLEM: Naive string search is O(N*M). When scanning terabytes of logs for fraud patterns, this latency is unacceptable.
SOLUTION: KMP Algorithm guarantees O(N) time by never backtracking. This catches fraud during the transaction, not after.

03. BIO-HAZARD (Graphs / BFS)

PROBLEM: Pollution isn't static; it spreads. Simple thresholds don't predict which neighborhood gets hit next.
SOLUTION: Modeling the city as a Graph (BFS) predicts the spread vector, allowing authorities to isolate clusters before air quality hits critical levels.

04. ENERGY GRID (MST / Kruskal)

PROBLEM: Connecting every house to every substation is economically infeasible due to cable costs.
SOLUTION: Kruskal‚Äôs MST guarantees the minimum-cost infrastructure while maintaining full connectivity (SDG 9).

05. ENERGY LOAD BALANCING (BFS + Greedy)

PROBLEM: During evening peak hours, some power feeders overload while others stay under-utilized, causing blackouts and equipment damage.
SOLUTION: A BFS-based feeder traversal combined with a Greedy load transfer redistributes excess power locally, preventing failures in real time (SDG 7).

06. GOVERNANCE (Priority Queue)

PROBLEM: Civic complaints pile up unevenly, with high-impact issues sometimes delayed and certain wards repeatedly ignored.
SOLUTION: A Priority Queue ranks grievances by severity, delay, and fairness score, ensuring urgent issues are handled first without regional bias (SDG 16).

07. SERVICE INEQUALITY (Hashing + Sorting)

PROBLEM: Some city wards consistently experience slower repairs, but inequality remains hidden in raw logs.
SOLUTION: Hash Maps aggregate repair data and sorting by average response time exposes systemic inequality, enabling data-driven governance reforms (SDG 10).
